Course 1: AI Foundations for Educational Innovation

← Back to Courses

Course Description

This course provides a foundational understanding of Artificial Intelligence (AI) concepts and their transformative potential within educational contexts. It explores key AI technologies, their applications in learning and teaching, and the ethical considerations surrounding their use. The course is designed for individuals with no prior programming experience, focusing on conceptual understanding and practical implications for educational innovation.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Define core AI concepts and terminology relevant to education.
  • Identify and describe various AI technologies applicable in educational settings.
  • Analyze the potential benefits and challenges of integrating AI into learning and teaching processes.
  • Evaluate ethical considerations and biases associated with AI in education.
  • Propose innovative applications of AI to address educational challenges.

Weekly Syllabus

Week 1: Introduction to AI in Education

Topics: What is AI? History and evolution of AI. AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning (conceptual overview). The promise and perils of AI in education. Current landscape of AI in educational technology.

Learning Activities: Discussions on personal experiences with AI, brainstorming potential AI applications in education.

Week 2: Core AI Concepts for Educators

Topics: Data and algorithms (without technical depth). Understanding how AI learns (training data, patterns). Introduction to natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision in educational contexts. Rule-based systems vs. learning systems.

Learning Activities: Case studies of AI in educational tools (e.g., intelligent tutoring systems, content recommendation).

Week 3: AI-Powered Learning Tools and Platforms

Topics: Adaptive learning platforms. AI in personalized learning paths. Chatbots and virtual assistants for student support. AI for content creation and curation. Examples of current EdTech AI tools.

Learning Activities: Exploring and reviewing existing AI-powered educational tools.

Week 4: AI for Teaching and Assessment

Topics: AI in automated grading and feedback. AI for identifying learning gaps. AI-assisted lesson planning and curriculum design. Predictive analytics for student success. Ethical considerations in AI assessment.

Learning Activities: Analyzing scenarios where AI can assist teachers, discussing the implications of AI in assessment.

Week 5: Ethical AI in Education

Topics: Bias in AI algorithms and data. Privacy concerns and data security. Transparency and explainability in AI. The role of human oversight in AI systems. Ensuring equitable access to AI in education.

Learning Activities: Debates on ethical dilemmas in AI education, developing guidelines for responsible AI use.

Week 6: AI and Learning Analytics

Topics: Introduction to learning analytics (conceptual). How AI enhances learning analytics. Using data to understand student engagement and performance. Ethical data collection and use in education.

Learning Activities: Interpreting learning analytics dashboards (simulated), discussing data-driven decision-making.

Week 7: Future Trends and Innovations in AI Education

Topics: Emerging AI technologies (e.g., generative AI, explainable AI). AI in virtual and augmented reality for learning. The future of the educator's role in an AI-integrated classroom. Preparing students for an AI-driven world.

Learning Activities: Visioning exercises for future educational landscapes, proposing novel AI applications.

Week 8: Capstone Project: AI Innovation Proposal

Topics: Synthesizing course knowledge. Developing a proposal for an AI-driven educational innovation. Presenting a problem, proposed AI solution, ethical considerations, and potential impact.

Learning Activities: Project work, peer feedback, final proposal presentations.
