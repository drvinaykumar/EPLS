Course 2: Developmental Psychology and Learning

← Back to Courses

Course Description

This course explores the fundamental theories and research in developmental psychology, focusing on how human development across the lifespan influences learning processes. It examines cognitive, social, emotional, and physical development from infancy through adulthood, and considers the implications of these developmental stages for educational practice and intervention.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Describe major theories of human development and their relevance to learning.
  • Analyze the interplay between cognitive, social, emotional, and physical development.
  • Identify developmental milestones and their impact on educational readiness.
  • Apply developmental principles to design age-appropriate learning environments and strategies.
  • Understand individual differences in development and their implications for inclusive education.

Weekly Syllabus

Week 1: Foundations of Developmental Psychology

Topics: Introduction to developmental psychology. Key theories: Piaget, Vygotsky, Erikson. Nature vs. Nurture debate. Research methods in developmental psychology.

Learning Activities: Discussion of personal developmental experiences, analyzing theoretical perspectives on development.

Week 2: Cognitive Development: Infancy and Early Childhood

Topics: Piaget's stages of cognitive development (sensorimotor, preoperational). Information processing theory. Language acquisition. Early childhood education implications.

Learning Activities: Case studies of cognitive development in young children, observing early learning behaviors.

Week 3: Cognitive Development: Middle Childhood and Adolescence

Topics: Piaget's concrete operational and formal operational stages. Development of memory, attention, and problem-solving skills. Adolescent brain development. Implications for middle and high school education.

Learning Activities: Discussing cognitive challenges in adolescence, analyzing learning strategies for different age groups.

Week 4: Social and Emotional Development

Topics: Erikson's psychosocial stages. Attachment theory. Development of self-concept and identity. Peer relationships and social learning. Emotional regulation and its impact on learning.

Learning Activities: Role-playing social scenarios, discussing the importance of emotional intelligence in education.

Week 5: Moral Development and Prosocial Behavior

Topics: Kohlberg's stages of moral development. Gilligan's critique. Development of empathy and altruism. Promoting ethical behavior in educational settings.

Learning Activities: Analyzing moral dilemmas, discussing strategies for fostering prosocial behavior.

Week 6: Physical Development and Health in Learning

Topics: Brain development and plasticity. Motor skill development. Impact of nutrition, sleep, and physical activity on learning. Addressing health disparities in education.

Learning Activities: Discussing the role of physical activity in cognitive function, exploring health education initiatives.

Week 7: Atypical Development and Inclusive Education

Topics: Introduction to developmental disorders (e.g., ADHD, autism spectrum disorder). Learning disabilities. Strategies for supporting diverse learners. Principles of inclusive education.

Learning Activities: Case studies of students with diverse learning needs, discussing differentiated instruction.

Week 8: Lifespan Development and Educational Implications

Topics: Development in early and middle adulthood. Learning in later life. Intergenerational learning. Applying developmental theories to adult education and professional development.

Learning Activities: Reflecting on lifelong learning, designing educational programs for different adult age groups.

Readings and Resources

Extensive readings covering developmental theories from Piaget, Vygotsky, and Erikson, along with contemporary research on cognitive, social, and emotional development across the lifespan.
