Course 4: Assessment and Measurement in Education

← Back to Courses

Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of educational assessment and measurement principles. It covers various types of assessments, their design, implementation, and interpretation, with a focus on ensuring validity, reliability, and fairness. Students will learn to critically evaluate assessment tools and use data to inform instructional decisions and promote student learning.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Differentiate between various types of educational assessments (formative, summative, diagnostic, norm-referenced, criterion-referenced).
  • Apply principles of validity, reliability, and fairness in assessment design and evaluation.
  • Interpret assessment data to inform instructional planning and student support.
  • Design and develop appropriate assessment tools for diverse learning objectives and student populations.
  • Understand the ethical and legal considerations in educational assessment.

Weekly Syllabus

Week 1: Introduction to Educational Assessment

Topics: Purpose of assessment in education. Types of assessment: formative, summative, diagnostic. Traditional vs. authentic assessment. The role of assessment in the learning process.

Learning Activities: Discussion on the importance of assessment, analyzing different assessment purposes.

Week 2: Principles of Measurement: Validity

Topics: Definition of validity. Types of validity: content, criterion-related (predictive, concurrent), construct. Threats to validity. Ensuring validity in assessment design.

Learning Activities: Evaluating assessment items for content validity, discussing challenges in establishing construct validity.

Week 3: Principles of Measurement: Reliability

Topics: Definition of reliability. Types of reliability: test-retest, inter-rater, internal consistency. Factors affecting reliability. Strategies for improving reliability.

Learning Activities: Calculating simple reliability coefficients (conceptual understanding), analyzing sources of measurement error.

Week 4: Fairness and Bias in Assessment

Topics: Definition of fairness in assessment. Sources of bias: cultural, linguistic, socioeconomic. Strategies for reducing bias. Accommodations and modifications for diverse learners.

Learning Activities: Critically analyzing assessment items for potential bias, discussing ethical considerations in assessment.

Week 5: Designing Effective Assessments

Topics: Developing learning objectives (Bloom's Taxonomy). Constructing various item types: multiple-choice, true/false, matching, short answer, essay. Rubric development for performance assessments.

Learning Activities: Designing assessment items for specific learning objectives, developing a rubric for a project-based assessment.

Week 6: Interpreting Assessment Data

Topics: Basic descriptive statistics (mean, median, mode, standard deviation). Norm-referenced vs. criterion-referenced interpretation. Using assessment data for instructional decision-making. Introduction to data visualization for assessment.

Learning Activities: Analyzing sample assessment data, discussing how data can inform teaching strategies.

Week 7: Large-Scale Assessment and Accountability

Topics: Standardized testing. High-stakes assessment. The role of assessment in educational policy and accountability. Criticisms and controversies surrounding large-scale assessments.

Learning Activities: Debating the pros and cons of standardized testing, analyzing the impact of accountability systems.

Week 8: Portfolio Assessment and Authentic Assessment

Topics: Principles of portfolio assessment. Designing and implementing portfolios. Project-based learning and assessment. Integrating authentic assessment into the curriculum. Capstone assessment project.

Learning Activities: Developing a plan for implementing portfolio assessment, presenting an authentic assessment design.
