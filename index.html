Master of Science in Educational Psychology and Learning Science

Equip yourself with comprehensive understanding of how individuals learn, develop, and interact within educational contexts. Integrate principles from psychology, cognitive science, and educational technology to foster innovative approaches to teaching and learning.

Program Overview

This Master of Science (MSc) program in Educational Psychology and Learning Science is designed to equip students with a comprehensive understanding of how individuals learn, develop, and interact within educational contexts. The curriculum integrates principles from psychology, cognitive science, and educational technology to foster innovative approaches to teaching, learning, and educational leadership.

8 Core Courses

Comprehensive curriculum covering AI foundations, developmental psychology, cognitive science, and educational leadership

Flexible Certification

Choose from individual certificates, postgraduate diploma, or full MSc degree

No Programming Required

Focus on theoretical knowledge and practical applications without technical prerequisites

Research Opportunities

Optional thesis project with GenAI-assisted research methodology

About the Program

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply psychological theories to educational practice and policy
  • Analyze learning processes across diverse populations and contexts
  • Design evidence-based educational interventions and assessments
  • Integrate AI and technology tools in educational settings
  • Lead educational change and innovation initiatives
  • Conduct research in educational psychology and learning science

Program Structure

Duration: 8-12 months

Format: Online with interactive sessions

Credits: 60 ECTS

Target Audience

Educators, school administrators, educational consultants, and professionals seeking to advance their understanding of learning and development.
